# Indian Insurance Basics

Life insurance in India is mainly for income replacement, not investment returns.

## Product categories
- Term insurance: pure protection, usually highest cover for lowest premium.
- Endowment and money-back: combine savings with lower protection efficiency.
- ULIP: market-linked value with insurance, more complexity and lock-in.
- Whole-life: legacy-oriented, useful for estate planning use-cases.

## What each type is best for
- Term: protecting spouse/children from loss of earning income.
- Endowment/money-back: forced savings goals with lower insurance efficiency.
- ULIP: long-term investors comfortable with market ups and downs.
- Whole-life: legacy transfer and estate intent.

## Practical guidance
- A 10x annual income benchmark is a rule of thumb, not personalized advice.
- Families with loans, young children, or dependent parents may need higher cover.
- Keep nominee details updated and share policy info with family.
- Claim settlement ratio is useful context, but not a guarantee for any individual claim.

## Key India-specific insurance terms
- Free-look period: review window after policy issuance to cancel with defined deductions.
- Grace period: additional time to pay premium before policy lapses.
- Surrender value: amount payable if eligible policy is exited early.
- Waiting period (health plans): period before some benefits become payable.
- Exclusions: situations that are not covered by the policy wording.

## Riders (add-ons)
- Accidental death benefit rider
- Critical illness rider
- Waiver of premium rider
- Income benefit rider

Riders can improve protection, but they should not replace adequate base life cover.

## Claim process basics
1. Inform insurer quickly and collect required documents.
2. Submit claim form, policy details, identity documents, and bank details.
3. Insurer reviews documents and may ask for clarifications.
4. Decision and payout are processed after verification.

Common reasons for claim issues:
- non-disclosure of medical or lifestyle details
- lapsed policy due to unpaid premium
- nominee mismatch or outdated records
