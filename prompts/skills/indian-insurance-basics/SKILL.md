# Indian Insurance Basics

Use this skill to answer detailed educational questions about life insurance in India. Keep answers practical, clear, and non-salesy.

## Boundaries

- Explain concepts and tradeoffs.
- Do not recommend a specific insurer or plan.
- Do not quote exact premiums as if they are guaranteed.
- Do not give tax, legal, or claim-dispute advice.
- If the user asks what to buy, explain the categories and suggest a licensed adviser for the final decision.

## 1. Main life insurance categories

### Term insurance
- Pure protection cover.
- Pays the nominee if the insured dies during the policy term.
- Usually the most cost-efficient way to buy high death cover.
- Best fit when the goal is income replacement for dependents.

### Endowment policy
- Mixes insurance with savings.
- Pays on death or on maturity.
- Usually offers much lower cover than a term plan for the same premium.
- Can count toward existing life cover, but is less efficient as a protection product.

### ULIP
- Mixes insurance with market-linked investing.
- Returns depend on fund performance.
- Has a 5-year lock-in period.
- Counts toward existing cover, but is more complex than pure protection products.

### Whole-life insurance
- Cover can continue for life, often to age 99 or 100.
- More relevant for estate transfer or legacy goals than everyday income replacement.

### Money-back policy
- Variant of an endowment policy with periodic payouts during the policy term.
- Usually behaves more like a savings product than a high-cover protection product.

## 2. Important terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Sum assured | The amount paid on death or maturity, depending on policy type |
| Premium | Amount paid to keep the policy active |
| Policy term | How long the cover lasts |
| Nominee | Person registered to receive the claim amount |
| Claim settlement ratio | Share of claims settled by the insurer in a reporting period; useful, but not a guarantee |
| Free-look period | Short window after policy issuance when the buyer can review and return the policy |
| Surrender value | Amount paid if an eligible policy is exited early |
| Grace period | Extra time to pay premium before lapse |
| Lapsed policy | Policy that stopped due to unpaid premium |

## 3. Riders

Common riders in India include:

- Accidental death benefit
- Critical illness rider
- Waiver of premium
- Income benefit rider
- Additional term rider

Use riders as optional add-ons, not as substitutes for adequate base cover.

## 4. Claims process in India

Typical flow:

1. Notify the insurer quickly.
2. Gather documents such as death certificate, policy document, claimant ID, bank details, and medical or police records if relevant.
3. Submit the claim form and supporting documents.
4. The insurer may investigate early claims.
5. Settlement happens after document review and any investigation.

Common claim problems:

- non-disclosure of health history
- lapsed policy due to unpaid premium
- outdated nominee details
- policy exclusions

## 5. IRDAI and regulation

- IRDAI regulates insurance companies in India.
- IRDAI publishes consumer and insurer information such as annual reports.
- Users can raise grievances through official IRDAI channels and the Insurance Ombudsman framework.

## 6. Health insurance versus life insurance

| Topic | Life insurance | Health insurance |
|---|---|---|
| Main purpose | Income replacement for family | Medical expense protection |
| Payout recipient | Usually nominee | Usually policyholder or hospital |
| Trigger | Death, and sometimes maturity | Hospitalization or specified illness |

## 7. Explaining the 10x income rule

SecuFi uses `10 x annual income` as a simple benchmark for life cover adequacy.

When explaining it:

- call it a rule of thumb, not a guaranteed answer
- mention that loans, young children, and dependent parents can increase the need
- mention that a licensed adviser can do a more personalized calculation

## 8. Common mistakes families make

- assuming savings-oriented policies automatically solve protection needs
- buying too little cover
- forgetting to update nominees
- letting policies lapse
- not telling family members where policy information is kept
